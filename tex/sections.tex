\section{Problem Statement, Research Question, and Contributions}\label{sec:introduction}
Autonomous driving systems rely on dense LiDAR sensing, but raw point-cloud throughput quickly exceeds practical storage and communication budgets. This thesis studies whether compression can preserve \emph{task-relevant} scene content, rather than only minimizing global reconstruction error.

The core research question is: \emph{Can importance-aware latent quantization improve ROI-relevant reconstruction under constrained bitrate compared with uniform quantization?}

For readability, this manuscript reports four model families: \textbf{Uniform Baseline (UB)}, \textbf{Adaptive ROI Student (ARS)}, \textbf{Adaptive Distilled Student (ADS)}, and \textbf{Multi-Scale Distilled Student (MS-ADS)}. Internal engineering stage names are treated as implementation details and omitted from the main narrative.

This work contributes: (1) an end-to-end ROI-aware LiDAR range-image compression framework; (2) a loss-design analysis that links objective terms to observed failure modes; and (3) a bottleneck decomposition that separates quantizer potential from importance-map quality.

\section{Related Work}\label{sec:related_work}

\subsection{Learned LiDAR Compression}
Classical LiDAR coding pipelines provide strong lossless baselines, but they are not optimized for task-aware distortion allocation~\cite{isenburg2013laszip}. Learned methods improve rate--distortion behavior by modeling geometric regularity in projected representations~\cite{wang2022rangeentropy,liu2024octreedepth}. Temporal predictive coding further reduces redundancy by encoding inter-frame innovation rather than fully independent frames~\cite{fan2024umotion,sun2025lpcm}.

\subsection{ROI- and Task-Aware Compression}
Recent task-aware codecs emphasize semantic importance and downstream utility. ROI-guided approaches show that allocating finer quantization to safety-critical regions can preserve perception quality under equal budget~\cite{xie2024roipcgc}. More broadly, scene-aware coding studies report that geometric fidelity alone does not guarantee downstream robustness, motivating objective terms tied to task behavior~\cite{sun2023salpcc,martins2024impact}.

\subsection{Distillation-Oriented Guidance}
Teacher-guided training is often used to transfer richer priors into compact students. In this thesis, distillation is not treated as an end in itself; instead, it is evaluated as a mechanism for improving importance-map calibration and ROI selectivity under aggressive compression.

\subsection{Comparison to PointCompress3D}
PointCompress3D presents a systems-level framework for roadside LiDAR compression and streaming, emphasizing deployment throughput and integrated codec evaluation~\cite{zimmer2024pointcompress3d}. The present thesis is complementary: it focuses on objective design inside a trainable adaptive quantization pipeline and analyzes how loss formulation affects ROI preservation.

\section{Methodology and Model Families}\label{sec:method}

\subsection{Pipeline}
Each LiDAR scan is projected to a five-channel range image
\begin{equation}
\mathbf{x} \in \mathbb{R}^{B\times5\times H\times W},
\end{equation}
and processed as
\begin{equation}
\mathbf{x} \xrightarrow{\text{encoder}} \mathbf{z} \xrightarrow{\text{importance head}} \mathbf{p} \xrightarrow{\text{adaptive quantizer}} \hat{\mathbf{z}} \xrightarrow{\text{decoder}} \hat{\mathbf{x}}.
\label{eq:pipeline}
\end{equation}

The encoder backbone is ResNet-style or DarkNet-style, with latent width 64. In MS-ADS, multi-scale encoder features are additionally consumed by the importance branch.

\subsection{Adaptive Quantization}
Importance probabilities \(\mathbf{p}\in[0,1]\) control local quantization levels:
\begin{equation}
\ell = b_{\mathrm{bg}} + \mathbf{p}\,(b_{\mathrm{roi}}-b_{\mathrm{bg}}),
\label{eq:level-map}
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
\tilde{\ell}=\max(2,\mathrm{round}(\ell)).
\label{eq:rounded-level}
\end{equation}
In this design, larger importance means finer local quantization (more levels), so ROI regions are preserved more aggressively than background.

\subsection{Model Families}
\textbf{UB} uses uniform quantization without ROI guidance. \textbf{ARS} introduces adaptive quantization with ROI supervision. \textbf{ADS} adds teacher-guided distillation to ARS. \textbf{MS-ADS} keeps the ADS objective and replaces the ROI branch with stronger multi-scale designs.

\section{Objective and Loss Design}\label{sec:loss}

\subsection{Unified Objective}
With reconstruction \(\hat{\mathbf{x}}\), importance logits \(\mathbf{s}\), ROI target \(\mathbf{m}\), and level map \(\tilde{\ell}\), training minimizes
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{total}}=
\lambda_{\mathrm{recon}}\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{recon}}+
\lambda_{\mathrm{distill}}\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{distill}}+
\lambda_{\mathrm{rate}}\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{rate}}+
\lambda_{\mathrm{imp}}\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{imp}}+
\lambda_{\mathrm{sep}}\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{sep}}.
\label{eq:total-objective}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Reconstruction Term}
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{recon}}=\mathrm{MSE}(\hat{\mathbf{x}},\mathbf{x}).
\label{eq:recon-loss}
\end{equation}
This term preserves global signal fidelity and anchors all families, including UB.

\subsection{Rate Proxy Term}
Legacy rate regularization uses
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{rate}}^{\mathrm{global}}=\mathrm{mean}(\tilde{\ell}).
\label{eq:rate-global}
\end{equation}
which can suppress importance if over-weighted. To reduce this conflict, normalized forms are used:
\begin{equation}
\ell_{\mathrm{norm}}=\frac{\tilde{\ell}-b_{\mathrm{bg}}}{b_{\mathrm{roi}}-b_{\mathrm{bg}}},
\label{eq:rate-norm-def}
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{rate}}^{\mathrm{norm\mbox{-}global}}=\mathrm{mean}(\ell_{\mathrm{norm}}),
\label{eq:rate-norm-global}
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{rate}}^{\mathrm{norm\mbox{-}bg}}=\frac{\sum(\ell_{\mathrm{norm}}\odot w_{\mathrm{bg}})}{\sum w_{\mathrm{bg}}}.
\label{eq:rate-norm-bg}
\end{equation}
The background-weighted variant reduces pressure on ROI locations while still regularizing bitrate growth.

\subsection{Importance Supervision Term}
Importance is supervised with weighted BCE:
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{imp}}=\mathrm{BCEWithLogits}(\mathbf{s},\mathbf{m};\mathrm{pos\_weight}),
\label{eq:imp-bce}
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
\mathrm{pos\_weight}=\mathrm{clip}\!\left(\frac{N_{\mathrm{neg}}}{\max(N_{\mathrm{pos}},1)},1,w_{\max}\right).
\label{eq:imp-posweight}
\end{equation}
This term addresses severe ROI class imbalance after downsampling.

\subsection{ROI--Background Separation Term}
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{sep}}=\max\left(0,\delta-(\mu_{\mathrm{roi}}-\mu_{\mathrm{bg}})\right),
\label{eq:sep-loss}
\end{equation}
where \(\mu_{\mathrm{roi}}\) and \(\mu_{\mathrm{bg}}\) are mean importance over ROI and background. The intent is better decision-margin separation under fixed thresholding.

\subsection{Distillation Term}
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{distill}}=\alpha_f\mathcal{L}_f+\alpha_{\ell}\mathcal{L}_{\ell},
\label{eq:distill-total}
\end{equation}
combining feature and logit alignment. For single-channel logits, BCE-style alignment is preferred over KL to avoid degenerate channel-wise behavior.

\subsection{Why This Loss Design}
Loss choices were driven by failure analysis. Collapse cases (empty ROI predictions at standard threshold) motivated replacing raw global rate pressure with normalized variants. ROI sparsity motivated weighted BCE. FP-heavy regimes motivated ROI--background separation. Native-vs-oracle analysis then confirmed that quantization capacity exists, while importance quality remains the limiting factor.

\section{Experimental Setup (Snapshot: February 20, 2026)}\label{sec:setup}
Training uses SemanticKITTI range-image projections with latent width 64 and adaptive levels \(b_{\mathrm{roi}}=256\), \(b_{\mathrm{bg}}=16\). ARS uses normalized-global rate with weighted BCE; ADS/MS-ADS use background-weighted normalized rate with weighted BCE and separation margin. Distillation uses a pretrained PointPillars-based teacher.

We report two evaluation regimes: (1) full 150-epoch sweeps for convergence behavior and objective-level comparison; (2) 16-frame diagnostics for quick quality and ROI-overlap interpretation.

\subsection{Preliminary MS-ADS Results}
MS-ADS remains preliminary. In the current 150-epoch snapshot, the best variant in both backbones uses a dynamic multi-scale head, with final objective 1.3593 (ResNet) and 2.1863 (DarkNet). These values are encouraging, but detector-level validation and threshold calibration are not yet complete.

\section{Discussion and Limitations}\label{sec:discussion}
The experiments support three practical conclusions. First, loss redesign substantially improved training stability relative to collapse-prone settings. Second, distillation is useful only in a calibrated range; excessive distillation weight degrades outcomes. Third, oracle decomposition shows large untapped efficiency, implying that improving ROI estimation quality is currently the highest-value direction.

Two limitations remain. Fixed-threshold behavior is still unstable across runs, so objective values alone are insufficient for deployment claims. In addition, MS-ADS conclusions are preliminary until detector-facing validation is complete.

%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%%
\section{Conclusion and Next Steps}\label{sec:conclusion}
This thesis demonstrates that ROI-aware adaptive quantization is a viable direction for LiDAR compression, but practical gains are presently constrained by importance-map calibration rather than quantizer capacity. Confirmed UB/ARS/ADS results and preliminary MS-ADS outcomes collectively point to a clear next step: prioritize robust ROI calibration and detector-level operating-point validation.
